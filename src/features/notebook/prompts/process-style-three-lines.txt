## Style: Three Lines of Defence

Render the process through the lens of the **Three Lines of Defence (3LoD)** model — the governance framework used by banks, insurers, and asset managers (IIA position paper, BCBS guidance) to demonstrate independent risk oversight.

Conventions:
- **Exactly three primary lanes**, in this order top→bottom:
  1. **1st Line — Business / Operations** (the risk owner: front office, operations, IT delivery).
  2. **2nd Line — Risk & Compliance** (independent risk management, compliance, model risk, security).
  3. **3rd Line — Internal Audit** (independent assurance).
- Optionally include a *Customer / External* lane on top and an *Executive / Board* lane at the very bottom for escalation paths.
- Place each task in the lane of the function that performs it. **Reviews and approvals belong in the lane of the reviewing function, not the requester** — that is the whole point of the model.
- Use `decision` for **control gates**: 1L self-check, 2L challenge, 3L audit finding. Each must have at least 2 labelled outgoing edges ("pass" / "fail", "approve" / "reject", "open finding" / "no finding").
- Use `task` with `control` set for every independent control activity (e.g. `"2LoD challenge — pricing model"`, `"3LoD audit — KYC sampling"`).
- Use `event` for periodic / scheduled triggers ("quarterly attestation due", "annual audit cycle").
- Use `data` for shared records (risk register, control library, audit findings tracker) — sits in a *Systems* lane at the bottom.

Edges:
- Sequence flow within a line.
- Edges crossing the line boundaries = **escalations or independent reviews** — make them explicit.

Outcomes (`end` nodes):
- *Risk accepted / closed*, *Finding raised*, *Escalated to Board / Risk Committee*, *Issue remediated*, etc.

Use this style when the source emphasises **governance, segregation, regulatory oversight, or audit findings** rather than transaction processing.
