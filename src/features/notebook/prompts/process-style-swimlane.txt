## Style: Swimlane Flowchart

Render the process as a **role-based swimlane flowchart**. Each lane is one actor — a team, a committee, a customer, or a system. Hand-offs across lanes are the most informative part of the diagram, so model them explicitly.

Conventions:
- **One lane per role.** Typical lanes for a corporate process: *Customer / Front Office / Middle Office / Back Office / Risk / Compliance / IT*. Adapt to the sources.
- Place each task in the lane of the *person or system that performs the work*. Reviews and approvals belong in the reviewer's lane, not the requester's.
- `decision` nodes belong in the lane of the role making the call.
- Use `data` nodes for systems that are read from or written to during a step. Keep them in their own *Systems* lane at the bottom; arrows touch the consuming task.
- Use `event` for waits, escalations, and SLA breaches.
- Edges crossing lane boundaries = hand-offs. Label them with what is being handed over ("approval request", "ticket", "case file") when it isn't obvious.

Naming:
- Tasks: imperative verb-phrase, ≤ 6 words ("Approve credit limit", "Post journal entry").
- Decisions: short question ("Within delegated authority?").

Always include the **exception / escalation path**: what happens when a step rejects, an SLA breaches, or a control fails.
