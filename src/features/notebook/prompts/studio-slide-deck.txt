## Visual Style (apply consistently to EVERY slide)

All slides MUST share a single, cohesive visual identity. Define it once and repeat it exactly:

- **Background:** soft off-white (#F5F2EB) with a subtle paper-like grain texture
- **Accent color:** warm coral/salmon (#E8735A) for highlights, underlines, and decorative elements
- **Title font style:** bold, slightly imperfect hand-drawn lettering — like a confident marker on a whiteboard
- **Body font style:** clean handwritten style, relaxed but legible, in dark charcoal (#2D2D2D)
- **Layout:** generous whitespace, left-aligned text, asymmetric placement that feels organic rather than rigid
- **Decorative elements:** small hand-drawn doodles (arrows, circles, underlines, simple icons) in coral or light gray as accents — keep them consistent across slides
- **Bullet points:** small hand-drawn dots or dashes, not perfect circles
- **Data visualizations:** hand-drawn style charts and graphs when applicable, with the coral accent color

This creates a modern "sketch-notes" or "whiteboard" aesthetic — approachable, memorable, and visually consistent.

1. First, the slide content in markdown (title, bullet points, notes) for the text view.
